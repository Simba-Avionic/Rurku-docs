\section{Architecture conventions}

\guidance{Keep IDs stable. When a block moves owner, update the table --- do not
silently invent a new name.}

\subsection{Block ID scheme}

\noindent\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Prefix} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\texttt{SYS-} & System-level / vehicle context \\
\texttt{PROP-} & Propulsion (chamber, injector, ignition) \\
\texttt{FEED-} & Tanks, feed lines, onboard valves \\
\texttt{STR-} & Structures / aerostructure \\
\texttt{REC-} & Recovery hardware \\
\texttt{AV-} & Avionics / onboard electronics \\
\texttt{GSE-} & Ground support equipment \\
\texttt{IF-} & Named interface (summary; detail $\rightarrow$ ICD) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\subsection{Figures}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Store under \texttt{figures/}; reference with
    \texttt{\textbackslash includegraphics}.
  \item Prefer PDF/SVG for diagrams; PNG for photos of hardware.
  \item Caption must state revision / date if the figure is a living sketch.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Change rule}

Architecture boundary changes (new subsystem, moved ownership, new primary
interface) require a SAD revision note in the changelog and a check against
affected SRD IDs / CONOPS procedures.
